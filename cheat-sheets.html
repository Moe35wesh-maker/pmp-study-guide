üìä Earned Value Management (EVM) Formulas

Core Values

PV (Planned Value)
PV = Planned % Complete √ó BAC
The budgeted cost of work scheduled to be completed by a specific date.
EV (Earned Value)
EV = Actual % Complete √ó BAC
The budgeted cost of work actually completed.
AC (Actual Cost)
AC = Total costs incurred to date
The actual cost incurred for work completed.
BAC (Budget at Completion)
BAC = Total project budget
The total planned budget for the project.

Variance Analysis

SV (Schedule Variance)
SV = EV - PV
Positive: Ahead of schedule
Negative: Behind schedule
Zero: On schedule
CV (Cost Variance)
CV = EV - AC
Positive: Under budget
Negative: Over budget
Zero: On budget

Performance Indices

SPI (Schedule Performance Index)
SPI = EV / PV
> 1.0: Ahead of schedule
< 1.0: Behind schedule
= 1.0: On schedule
CPI (Cost Performance Index)
CPI = EV / AC
> 1.0: Under budget
< 1.0: Over budget
= 1.0: On budget

Forecasting

EAC (Estimate at Completion)
EAC = BAC / CPI
Most common formula. Use when current variances are expected to continue.
ETC (Estimate to Complete)
ETC = EAC - AC
How much more money is needed to complete the project.
VAC (Variance at Completion)
VAC = BAC - EAC
Positive: Under budget at completion
Negative: Over budget at completion
TCPI (To-Complete Performance Index)
TCPI = (BAC - EV) / (BAC - AC)
The CPI needed for remaining work to stay on budget.

Other Important Formulas

Communication Channels
Channels = n(n - 1) / 2
Where n = number of people
Example:
5 people: 5(4)/2 = 10 communication channels
PERT (Three-Point Estimate)
Expected Duration = (O + 4M + P) / 6
O = Optimistic estimate
M = Most Likely estimate
P = Pessimistic estimate
Example:
O=3, M=5, P=9 ‚Üí (3 + 4(5) + 9) / 6 = 32/6 = 5.33 days
Standard Deviation
SD = (P - O) / 6
Measure of uncertainty in estimates

üéØ Quick Memory Tip

Remember "EVAC": EV comes first in all formulas!

  • SV = EV - PV
  • CV = EV - AC
  • SPI = EV / PV
  • CPI = EV / AC
üìù Complete EVM Example:
Given:
‚Ä¢ BAC = $100,000
‚Ä¢ Planned to be 60% complete (PV)
‚Ä¢ Actually 50% complete (EV)
‚Ä¢ Spent $55,000 (AC)

Calculate:
‚Ä¢ PV = 0.60 √ó $100,000 = $60,000
‚Ä¢ EV = 0.50 √ó $100,000 = $50,000
‚Ä¢ AC = $55,000

‚Ä¢ SV = $50,000 - $60,000 = -$10,000 (Behind schedule)
‚Ä¢ CV = $50,000 - $55,000 = -$5,000 (Over budget)
‚Ä¢ SPI = $50,000 / $60,000 = 0.83 (Behind)
‚Ä¢ CPI = $50,000 / $55,000 = 0.91 (Over budget)
‚Ä¢ EAC = $100,000 / 0.91 = $109,890
‚Ä¢ VAC = $100,000 - $109,890 = -$9,890 (Over budget)

üîÑ Agile & Scrum Quick Reference

Scrum Framework (3-5-3)

3 Roles

  1. Product Owner: Maximizes product value, manages Product Backlog
  2. Scrum Master: Servant leader, removes impediments, coaches team
  3. Development Team: Self-organizing, cross-functional, 3-9 members

5 Events

  1. Sprint: Time-box of 1 month or less (typically 2 weeks)
  2. Sprint Planning: Plan sprint work (8 hours for 1-month sprint)
  3. Daily Scrum: 15-minute daily standup
  4. Sprint Review: Inspect increment, adapt backlog (4 hours max)
  5. Sprint Retrospective: Team improvement meeting (3 hours max)

3 Artifacts

  1. Product Backlog: Ordered list of everything needed (owned by PO)
  2. Sprint Backlog: PBI selected for sprint + plan to deliver
  3. Increment: Sum of all PBI completed + value of previous increments

4 Agile Manifesto Values

  • Individuals and interactions over processes and tools
  • Working software over comprehensive documentation
  • Customer collaboration over contract negotiation
  • Responding to change over following a plan

12 Agile Principles (Simplified)

  • 1. Satisfy customer through early/continuous delivery
  • 2. Welcome changing requirements
  • 3. Deliver working software frequently
  • 4. Business & developers work together daily
  • 5. Build projects around motivated individuals
  • 6. Face-to-face conversation is most efficient
  • 7. Working software is primary measure of progress
  • 8. Sustainable development pace
  • 9. Continuous attention to technical excellence
  • 10. Simplicity is essential
  • 11. Self-organizing teams
  • 12. Reflect and adjust regularly

üë• Leadership & Team Management

Tuckman's Team Development Model

Stage Characteristics PM Actions
Forming Polite, uncertain, testing Provide direction, set goals
Storming Conflict, competition, resistance Coach, facilitate, set norms
Norming Cooperation, cohesion, trust Support, encourage, delegate
Performing High performance, independence Empower, monitor, celebrate
Adjourning Task completion, disbanding Recognize, document lessons

5 Types of Power

  • Formal (Legitimate): Authority from position/title
  • Reward: Ability to give rewards/bonuses
  • Penalty (Coercive): Ability to punish
  • Expert: Based on skills and knowledge
  • Referent: Based on respect and admiration

üéØ Best Types of Power for PMs

Expert and Referent are the most effective for project managers!

Conflict Resolution Techniques

Technique Description Result
Withdraw/Avoid Retreat from conflict Lose-Lose
Smooth/Accommodate Emphasize agreement, minimize differences Lose-Win
Compromise/Reconcile Both parties give up something Lose-Lose
Force/Direct Push one viewpoint Win-Lose
Collaborate/Problem Solve Work together for best solution Win-Win (BEST)

Leadership Styles

  • Laissez-faire: Hands-off, let team self-manage
  • Transactional: Focus on rewards and penalties
  • Servant Leader: Focus on serving team needs (Agile)
  • Transformational: Inspire and motivate change
  • Charismatic: Use personality to inspire
  • Interactional: Mix of transactional, transformational, charismatic

Motivation Theories

Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs
From bottom to top:
1. Physiological (food, water, shelter)
2. Safety (security, stability)
3. Social (belonging, love)
4. Esteem (respect, recognition)
5. Self-Actualization (reach full potential)
Herzberg's Two-Factor Theory
Hygiene Factors: Prevent dissatisfaction (salary, working conditions)
Motivators: Create satisfaction (achievement, recognition, growth)
McGregor's Theory X and Theory Y
Theory X: People dislike work, need control and direction
Theory Y: People enjoy work, are self-motivated

Communication Types

Type Description When to Use

Informal Verbal Conversations, discussions Quick updates, team chats

Active Listening Techniques

  • Pay attention: Focus fully on speaker
  • Show you're listening: Nod, maintain eye contact
  • Provide feedback: Paraphrase, ask questions
  • Defer judgment: Don't interrupt
  • Respond appropriately: Honest, respectful
