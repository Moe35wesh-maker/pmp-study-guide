üîÑ Agile vs Predictive Questions

üìä Critical Exam Insight

Many questions will specify OR imply the project approach. Read carefully for clues like "iterative," "sprint," "adaptive," or "traditional," "waterfall," "predictive."

Agile/Adaptive Context Clues

  • Sprint, iteration, increment
  • Product Owner, Scrum Master
  • Daily standup, retrospective
  • User stories, backlog
  • Self-organizing team
  • Servant leadership

Predictive/Traditional Context Clues

  • Detailed upfront planning
  • Gantt charts, critical path
  • Change control board
  • Baseline management
  • Formal approval processes

Decision Framework

In Agile Context, Favor Answers With:

  • Team empowerment and self-organization
  • Frequent customer collaboration
  • Embracing change
  • Working software over documentation
  • Servant leader removing impediments
  • Continuous improvement (kaizen)

In Predictive Context, Favor Answers With:

  • Following the project management plan
  • Change control processes
  • Comprehensive documentation
  • Variance analysis and corrective action
  • Formal approval and sign-offs
  • Baseline protection

üé≠ Common Exam Traps

Trap #1: The "Real World" Answer

The Trap

An answer choice reflects what you might do in your actual job, but violates PMI best practices.

The Solution

Choose the "PMI way," not the "real-world way." Follow processes, document everything, engage stakeholders.

Trap #2: The Incomplete Answer

The Trap

An answer is partially correct but skips important steps (like "review the risk register" without "then update it").

The Solution

Look for the MOST COMPLETE answer that includes all necessary steps.

Trap #3: All Answers Seem Right

The Trap

Multiple answers are technically correct, but one is BEST.

The Solution

Choose the most proactive, collaborative, process-following answer. When in doubt, pick the answer that involves more stakeholder engagement.

Trap #4: Sequence Confusion

The Trap

Question asks "what should the PM do FIRST" but lists activities from different process groups.

The Solution

Remember the sequence: Initiating ‚Üí Planning ‚Üí Executing ‚Üí M&C ‚Üí Closing. Always choose the earlier process group activity.

Trap #5: Over-Interpretation

The Trap

Reading too much into the question and making assumptions not stated in the scenario.

The Solution

Answer based ONLY on information given. Don't add your own assumptions.
